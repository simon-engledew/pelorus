Questions to keep asking

This section lists questions that stakeholders should be asking repeatedly and continuously, both of themselves and collaboratively with other stakeholders.

Obviously, any question where the answer is less than entirely satisfactory should trigger some action—perhaps a ‘formal’ report to a Goal Manager, but at very least a discussion with other stakeholders.

The duplication and redundancy amongst the questions is deliberate. A central principle of Pelorus is to have many different people looking at the map from many different perspectives, contributing both their individual knowledge and their collective wisdom.

For the map as a whole

Do I understand this map?

Am I comfortable with this map?

Does the map play to our strengths? Does it build on what we’re good at?

Do the head goals represent end benefits that are self-justifying?

Are the end benefits the ones we should be aiming for at this time?

Have there been any changes in the external environment that might cause us to reconsider any aspects of the map—whether the end benefits or the change paths?

Are we being ambitious enough?

Are we being too ambitious?

Are the change paths complete and aligned?

Do the change paths represent our best approach to achieving the end benefits?

Have we identified all stakeholders?

Are all stakeholders actively engaged?

Am I aware of any risks that haven’t been captured?

Do all identified risks have the right severity status?

What have I learned recently that justifies my continuing confidence?

What have I learned recently that gives me cause for concern?

What have I learned recently that might lead me to suggest changes to the map, whether minor or major?

For my own goal(s)

Do I understand this goal?

Am I comfortable with this goal?

Have we identified all stakeholders?

Are all stakeholders actively engaged?

Does the Goal Manager have the necessary authority to deliver the goal?

Is the goal defined with sufficient precision? (sub-goals and factors)

Is the status of the goal up-to-date and accurate? (predicted/actual factor values, risks)

Are factor target values realistic?

Am I confident that we can achieve the goal?

Am I aware of any risks that haven’t been captured?

Do all identified risks have the right severity status?

What have I learned recently that justifies my continuing confidence?

What have I learned recently that gives cause for concern?

What have I learned recently that might lead me to suggest changes to this goal, whether minor or major?

Looking at supporting goals

Do I understand our supporting goals?

Are they defined with sufficient precision? (sub-goals and factors)

Am I confident our supporting goals will be achieved? If not, why not?

Collectively, do the supporting goals provide all the foundations needed for achieving our own goal?

Are the supporting goals providing anything we don’t need?

Given what the supporting goals are providing, could we usefully do more or better at our own goal?

Looking at supported goals

Do I understand our supported goals?

Are they defined with sufficient precision? (sub-goals and factors)

Am I confident our supported goals will be achieved? If not, why not?

Does our goal meet the needs of our supported goals?

Is our goal providing anything our supported goals don’t need?

Given what our goal is providing, could our supported goals usefully do more or better?
